Your first app

It's time to create our first Flutter application!

Before we start coding, you should install an iPhone / Android simulator. You can choose whatever device you want, but it's important to consider the oldest OS version that our app supports. Since it's for test purpose, let's use something that won't go wrong by choosing a relatively new api level and device. A recommended one for Android is the "Resizable (Experimental)".

  1. Go to the nav bar on the rightmost side of AS, and click on "Device manager".
  2. choose "Resizable" and click "next".
  3. Choose API level of 33.
  4. As it's well known a simulator can really take some memory resources. So, you should give it enough memory for it to run. A recommended size is 12GB.
  5. Now, click on the yes options till it's being downloaded, and wait for a while till the download is done.
  6. Once you've successfully downloaded a device, restart AS.

Now, let's do some coding. On the leftmost side of the top nav bar, click and hover on "File".

  1. File > New Flutter Project
  2. Click on "Flutter" on the left. Specify Flutter SDK dir if necessary.
  3. Name your project, and put it somewhere you like. Click on "next". Remain other options as default
  4. Click "Finish"
  5. Wait until nothing pops up.

You should see this the file structure like this. You should be able to open up "lib > main.dart".


Navigate to the top nav bar. Run your device in "device manager". Once the device shows up, "run main.dart". You might need to manually select your device, please select the one you downloaded. When you first lunch your app, it may take a while.


Hot reload

One of the best feature of Flutter is that the app can hot reload itself. You don't need to restart the app to see that you've made. While your app is running, click on the little lightning button to hot reload your app.

We can that Flutter has already created the app that counts the number of times we click on the button on the bottom right. In the next section, we'll be introducing what the elements in the code are and what they do.
